My dad went on business trips frequently, many to Alaska in fact, and while he was gone it became a tradition for me and my mom to go tot he Sea Galley and eat together. I really liked the place, but we moved to Portland in 1986. Interestingly enough, one of the co-owners of Sea Galley started a second franchise called “Flakey Jakes”, right about 1983 or 1984. It was a burger place, the first “burger bar” ever. You could get 1/3 lb hot dogs and 1/2 lb burgers cooked to your liking, fries, and huge milk shakes. They had the best video games too and the atmosphere, unlike Sea Galley, was more like being apart of a country ho-down in a barn. We loved both places. Sadly, Flakey Jakes over franchised, just as Sea Galley did and suffered from the intrusion of a competitor named Fuddruckers, much like Sea Galley suffered from Red Lobster. We did find one last remaining Flakey Jakes in Danville, CA circa 1990 or so, but even that place closed. I’m glad to see Sea Galley is still alive in Yakima and Anchorage, I smell a road trip for piracy on the crab leg seas!

Good food, great family environment and just plain enjoyable. Truly an all around treat. And, a good bargain. And, a fun place to go for everybody. Every lunch and dinner, every day the place was packed, even during Seattle’s slow economy.

Then . . . the management went crazy, or got greedy, or something and decided to go “yuppie” (an 80′s term) by totally changing their decor, making it more “elegant”; changing the menu to include haute cuisine and postage stamp portions; took away the salad bar; made it too stuffy to bring kids; hiked the prices way up.

The first time we went into one of the remodeled units (Totem Lake) was the last time we walked-in the door. Within weeks of making these changes you could go into a Sea Galley on a Friday evening and shoot a cannon through the place without hitting anyone.

To my experience, no one has come close to duplicating Sea Galley. Unfortunate indeed!
